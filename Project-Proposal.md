# Project Proposal — Historical EV Market Growth Analysis in the USA

*BA780: Introduction to Data Analytics · Fall 2024 · Team A05*

## Problem Definition

Electric vehicles have grown in popularity since their introduction in the early 2010s. The USA
has been one of the top producing and consuming countries in this market since its birth. The EV
market is advancing rapidly in some parts of the world, such as Europe and China; however, the
American market is still far from reaching its full potential. By analyzing the data, we aim to
understand some of the reasons behind the current state of the electric car market in the United
States.

## Motivation

We are interested in observing market trends for EVs starting at a macroeconomic level and then
conducting sub-analyses on specific states and regions that provide key insights into this area of
the automotive industry. Charging stations, state/region, and vehicle purchases are some of the KPIs
we analyze.

## Data Sources

- **U.S. Department of Energy — Alternative Fueling Station Locations by State:**
  https://afdc.energy.gov/stations/states
- **Atlas EV Hub — State EV Registration Data:**
  https://www.atlasevhub.com/materials/state-ev-registration-data/

## Planned Analyses

**Foundational**
- Count of EVs by make, year over year, overall and by state
- Count of EV charging stations, year over year, overall and by state
- Count of all charging-station types (electric, hydrogen, etc.), year over year and by state

**Intermediate**
- Charging-station count by ZIP code, year over year
- Total EVs vs. number of EV charging stations available, year over year and by state
- Most popular EV models, year over year, by state and overall

**Challenging**
- Alternative-fuel vehicles (biodiesel, hydrogen, etc.) vs. fuel stations available, by state
- Compare the highest- and lowest-EV states by charging-station level and EV count, with historical
  EV policy research
- Identify the most and least convenient states to drive an EV, using state area to gauge how spread
  out charging stations are
